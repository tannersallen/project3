Set in the 41st millennium, Warhammer 40,000 is a tabletop wargame that pits two players using any one of a range of armies against each other, vying for control of the battlefield. The armies are made up of plastic miniatures that are built and painted by the players. There are a few different aspects of the hobby, including playing the board game, building and painting miniatures, and other media set in the grim darkness of the far future such as video games, books, and animated shorts.

Models and Painting

Building the models and painting them is one of the aspects of the hobby that attracts the most people. Many people who have no interest in playing the tabletop game participate in model painting. There are even painting competitions held by Games Workshop, or independent organizations.

The first step in the process is cutting the pieces of the model off the sprue, cleaning off any additional plastic or mouldlines, and gluing them together.

The next step is to prime the model (typically done with a dark color, like black) and then paint the model however you'd like it to be painted!

The baisc essentials you'll need to build your minis and paint them are simple! You just need some clippers to remove the pieces of your model from the sprue, some plastic cement or super glue to put it together, and a few paintbrushes and paints.

The Tabletop Game

In the tabletop game, players pit their armies against each other. There are five rounds in a game, with each player getting a turn each round. Each player's turn consists of five phases: Command, Move, Shoot, Charge, and Fight.

Command Phase

During the command phase, players survey the battlefield, implement special commands to their troops, and if any units in their army have lost models, that unit must take a "battle-shock test" in order to determine if they are still combat effective that turn.

Movement Phase

In the movement phase, you move and position your units across the board, in search of cover, firing lines, battlefield objectives, or close combat opportunities. Some units have special movement abilities, allowing them to fly, climb, or even teleport down to the battlefield from orbit.

Shooting Phase

During your shooting phase, your units take aim with their guns and open fire, artillery unlease their bombardments, and tanks fire heavy salvos on the enemy.

Charge Phase

The charge phase is when your units that are in range of the enemy rush in to engage the enemy in close quarters. Although, when your troops charge in, the enemy has a chance to fire on them.

Fight Phase

Finally, to finish your turn, your troops engage in close quarters combat with the enemy that are within range.

Books and Other Media

There is a massive range of media outside of the tabletop game, and model painting within the IP of Warhammer 40,000. There are numerous books, video games, and more recently animated shorts set within the far future.

"The Black Library" is GamesWorkshop's publishing company, and they have produced hundreds of books, with more coming out all the time. Popular series to read include: The Eisenhorn series, The Siege of Terra, and The Horus Heresy.

When it comes to video games, there are many to choose from. Probably the most well-known is the recently released "Space Marine 2". But there are many others including real-time strategy, first-person shooters, and role-playing games to choose from.

GamesWorkshop also now has a streaming service with regularly published videos of animated shorts, recorded tabletop games, and even painting tutorials.
